First and silly rhythms with the TE PO-12 (9/12/18)
    The Teenage Engineering PO-12 Rhythm Pocket Calculator is an amazing toy to experiment with rhythms. It is not a super professional drum machine, but more than enough for an amateur like me who just wants to learn. And for a pianist, it can be seen as well as an enhanced metronome. Are you bored of practising with an old metronome? Try the po-12 to have more fun. You can modulate the amount of swing, so it is ideal even for jazz!

    I will share here three very silly examples that I have created just to practice with the basics. And since only 16 patterns can be stored, this blog can be a good opportunity to store them in a permanent way by using an image file. For example, let's begin with a very simple one:

Menuet BWV 113 from the Album of Anna Magdalena Bach (Anonymous composer) (24/11/18)
    I have chosen the Album of Anna Magdalena Bach as my first recording material. With it, I want to learn how to record and edit tracks, and to experiment with different sounds. For now, all piano tracks come from the Royal Grand of the Nord Stage 3. I will postpone recording on my real grand piano until I learn more about how to properly set the microphones in the room.

    This is also a good exercise of studying a simple piece, recording it and letting it go. Otherwise, I feel the (obsessive) pressure of keeping it as part of my study and this prevents me from expanding my repertoire. Recording each piece is a nice way to remember that I could play them.
